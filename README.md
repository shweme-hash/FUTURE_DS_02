📊 Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard

The key question is:

“If I were the founder, what would I want to know from this analysis?”
This project focuses on identifying why customers leave (churn) and what actions can reduce it.

📌 Key Metrics

Total Customers: 7,043
Churned Customers: 1,869
Churn Rate: 27%
Churn Risk Level: High
👉 A 27% churn rate is a serious retention problem — growth will stall unless churn is reduced.

🔍 Key Insights & What They Mean
1. 📉 Month-to-Month Contracts Drive Churn
Customers on month-to-month contracts churn the most
Long-term contracts (1-year, 2-year) show significantly lower churn

💡 What the founder should do:
Introduce discounts or incentives for annual plans
Offer loyalty rewards after 3–6 months
Push “commitment upgrades” inside the app

3. 💳 Payment Method is a Hidden Risk
Customers using electronic check show higher churn
Automatic payments (bank/credit card) retain customers better

💡 Actionable strategy:
Encourage auto-pay adoption (discounts, convenience messaging)
Reduce friction in payment processes

4. 🌐 Internet Service Impacts Retention
Certain service types (like fiber vs DSL) show different churn behavior

💡 Founder insight:
Poor service experience = churn risk
Segment users by service quality and
prioritize support for high-risk groups

5. 💸 Monthly Charges & Churn Behavior
Customers with specific price ranges appear more frequently in churn

💡 What this means:
Pricing may not match perceived value
👉 Recommended actions:
Introduce tiered pricing models
Bundle features to increase perceived value
Offer targeted discounts to high-risk users

6. 👥 Gender is NOT a Key Driver
 
Churn is almost evenly split between male and female customers
💡 Conclusion:
Gender is not a useful segmentation variable
Focus on behavior, pricing, and contracts instead


8. ⚠️ Overall Retention Problem
 
Nearly 1 in 3 customers leave
💡 Founder-level concern:
This is not just a marketing issue — it's a product + pricing + experience problem

🚀 Business Recommendations (What I Would Tell the Founder)

🔹 Short-Term Fixes (Quick Wins)
Offer discounts for annual subscriptions
Promote auto-payment enrollment
Target month-to-month users with retention campaigns

🔹 Medium-Term Improvements

Build a churn prediction model (identify at-risk users early)
Introduce customer success outreach for high-risk segments
Improve onboarding experience for new users

🔹 Long-Term Strategy

Optimize pricing vs value perception
Improve product quality & customer experience
Implement loyalty programs

🧰 Tools Used

PowerBI (Dashboard)

🎯 What I Learned

Thinking like a business decision-maker

📢 Final Thought

A dashboard is only valuable if it answers:
“What should we do next?”
This analysis highlights that reducing churn is not about one fix —
it’s about improving contracts, pricing, and customer experience together.
